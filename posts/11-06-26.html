are sigmoidal functions \( \gamma \)-bounding activations?

Introduction

In the last post, we saw that [Lajoie & Mazzucato, 2025] demonstrated the expressive power of networks with random weights and learned biases — but their main theorem only works under hard constraints on the activation function: \( \gamma \)-bounding and bias-learning activations. In their work, only ReLU and the Heaviside step function were shown to satisfy these conditions. This post is dedicated to answering the open question raised in Remark 3 of the previous post.

My contributions:

  • Proved that bounded, continuous sigmoidal functions are \( \gamma \)-bounding activations.
  • Introduced the definition of quasi bias-learning functions and showed that they still satisfy the universal approximation theorem.
Sigmoidal Functions are Gamma-Bounding Activations

We first recall the definition of a \( \gamma \)-bounding activation:

Definition
An activation function \( \sigma \) is a \( \gamma \)-bounding activation if it allows universal approximation even when the individual parameters (matrix and vector parameters) are bounded by \( \gamma \). ReLU and the Heaviside step function are \( \gamma \)-parameter bounding activations for any \( \gamma > 0 \).

To prove this property for sigmoidal functions, we first show it for the Heaviside function, then use the universal approximation theorem to approximate sigmoidal functions by Heaviside functions.

Let \( \phi \) denote the Heaviside function:

\[ \phi(x) = \mathbf{1}_{x \geq 0} \]

Notice that \( \phi(a(x+b)) = \phi(x+b) \). Let \( f \) be continuous on \( [0,1]^d \). By the universal approximation theorem, there exist parameters \( w_i, b_i, a_i \) such that: \[ f(x) \approx \sum_{i=1}^n a_i \phi(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) \] Since \( n \) is finite, the \( w_i, b_i \) are real numbers, and there exists \( M \in \mathbb{R} \) bounding their norms. We can find \( u \in \mathbb{R} \) such that \( M/u \leq \gamma \), so: \[ f(x) \approx \sum_{i=1}^n a_i \phi\!\left(\left\langle u \cdot \frac{w_i}{u},\, x \right\rangle + u \cdot \frac{b_i}{u}\right) = \sum_{i=1}^n a_i \phi\!\left(\left\langle \frac{w_i}{u},\, x \right\rangle + \frac{b_i}{u}\right) \] which satisfies the \( \gamma \)-bounding condition. Thus the Heaviside function is a \( \gamma \)-bounding activation.

Now let \( \sigma \) be a bounded, continuous sigmoidal function, i.e. \[ \lim_{x \to -\infty} \sigma(x) = 0, \quad \lim_{x \to +\infty} \sigma(x) = 1. \] For any continuous \( f \) on \( [0,1]^d \) and \( \epsilon > 0 \), there exists a network such that: \[ \sup_{x \in [0,1]^d} \left\| f(x) - \sum_{i=1}^n a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) \right\| \leq \frac{\epsilon}{2} \] For each \( i \), let \( y_i = \langle w_i, x \rangle \) be bounded on a compact set. Since \( \sigma \) is continuous on a compact set, we can approximate it by a Heaviside network: there exist parameters \( w_i^{(k)}, b_i^{(k)}, N_i \) such that: \[ \sup_{y_i} \left\| \sigma(y_i + b_i) - \sum_{k=1}^{N_i} a_i^{(k)} \phi(w_i^{(k)} \cdot y_i + b_i^{(k)}) \right\| \leq \frac{\epsilon}{2n a_i} \] By the triangle inequality: \[ \sup_{x \in [0,1]^d} \left\| f(x) - \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{k=1}^{N_i} a_i a_i^{(k)} \phi(w_i^{(k)} \cdot y_i + b_i^{(k)}) \right\| \leq \epsilon \] Therefore, a sigmoidal network can be approximated by a Heaviside network which has the \( \gamma \)-bounding property. A bounded continuous sigmoidal function is a \( \gamma \)-bounding activation. \( \square \)

Quasi Bias-Learning Functions

We first recall the definition of a bias-learning activation:

Definition — Bias-Learning Activation
\( \sigma \) is called a bias-learning activation if it is continuous and there exists \( r \in \mathbb{R} \) such that \( \sigma(x) = 0 \) for all \( x < r \).

We extend this to a more general notion:

Definition — Quasi Bias-Learning Activation
\( \sigma \) is called a quasi bias-learning activation if it is continuous and for any \( \mu > 0 \) there exists \( r \in \mathbb{R} \) such that \( \sigma(x) < \mu \) for all \( x < r \).

Therefore, a bounded continuous sigmoidal function \( \sigma \) is both a \( \gamma \)-bounding activation and a quasi bias-learning function.

Let \( p_R \) denote the uniform distribution on \( [-R, R] \) with \( R > \gamma \). The main result is:

Theorem 1
Assume \( \sigma \) is a \( \gamma \)-bounding and quasi bias-learning activation, and let \( h : U \to \mathbb{R} \) be continuous on a compact \( U \subset \mathbb{R}^d \). Then for any \( \varepsilon > 0 \) and \( \delta \in (0,1) \), there exist a hidden-layer width \( m \in \mathbb{N} \) and bias vector \( b \in \mathbb{R}^m \) such that, with probability at least \( 1 - \delta \), a single hidden-layer neural network with each weight sampled from \( p_R \) approximates \( h \) with error less than \( \varepsilon \).

We reuse the proof technique from [Lajoie & Mazzucato, 2025] based on masked neural networks. Let \( M \in \{0,1\}^m \) be a mask; a masked neural network is: \[ y_m^M = \sum_{k=1}^m M_i a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) \] Let \( y_N(x, \theta) = \sum_{i=1}^N a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) \). For any \( \epsilon > 0 \), there exist \( n \) and parameters \( \theta^\star \) such that: \[ \sup_{x \in U} \| h(x) - y_n(x, \theta^\star) \| \leq \epsilon \] The parameters \( \theta = (a_1, \ldots, a_n, w_1, \ldots, w_n, b_1, \ldots, b_n) \) can be concatenated into a vector in \( \mathbb{R}^p \) with \( p = n(n+d+2) \).

Remark 1. \( \theta^\star \) is deterministic, whereas \( \theta^M \) contains a random component from the \( w_i \) sampled from the uniform distribution.
Lemma 1 — Lemma 3 of [Lajoie & Mazzucato, 2025]
For \( \theta^\star \) defined above and arbitrary \( \varepsilon > 0 \), \( \delta \in (0,1) \), we can find \( m > n \) such that with probability at least \( 1 - \delta \): there exists \( M \in \{0,1\}^m \) with only \( n \) non-zero elements such that \[ |\theta_i^\star - \theta_i^M| < \varepsilon \quad \forall\, i \in \{1, \ldots, p\}. \]
Lemma 2 — Lemma 1 of [Lajoie & Mazzucato, 2025]
Let \( h : U \to \mathbb{R} \) be continuous on compact \( U \subset \mathbb{R}^d \). For any \( \varepsilon > 0 \) and \( \delta \in (0,1) \), there exists a layer width \( m \in \mathbb{N} \) such that with probability at least \( 1 - \delta \): there exists \( M \in \{0,1\}^m \) satisfying: \[ \sup_{x \in U} \| h(x) - y_m^M(x) \| \leq \varepsilon. \]

The main proof idea proceeds in four steps:

  1. First, establish the existence of a deterministic neural network with learned weights and biases.
  2. Second, for sufficiently large \( m \), assign the \( m \) weight vectors to a uniform distribution.
  3. Third, since the distribution is uniform, some weights among the \( m \) will lie in the confidence interval of the "good" weights.
  4. Finally, mask the unnecessary weights using the quasi bias-learning property.
Proof of Theorem 1

Let \( \epsilon > 0 \) and \( \delta \in (0,1) \). By Lemma 2, there exists \( m \in \mathbb{N} \) such that with probability at least \( 1-\delta \), there exists \( M \in \{0,1\}^m \) with: \[ \sup_{x \in U} \| h(x) - y_m^M(x) \| \leq \epsilon/2 \] Let \( \mathcal{S} = \{i : M_i = 1\} \) so \( |\mathcal{S}| = n \). We write: \[ y(x) = \sum_{i \in \mathcal{S}} a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) + \sum_{i \notin \mathcal{S}} a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) \] Since \( \sigma \) is quasi bias-learning and the inputs are bounded, for each \( i \notin \mathcal{S} \) we can choose \( b_i \) such that: \[ |a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i)| = \frac{\epsilon}{2(m-n)} \] For \( i \in \mathcal{S} \), we keep \( b_i \) unchanged. Then: \[ y(x) = \sum_{i \in \mathcal{S}} a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i) + \frac{\epsilon}{2} \] By the triangle inequality: \begin{align*} \sup_{x \in U} \| h(x) - y(x) \| &\leq \sup_{x \in U} \| h(x) - y_m^M(x) \| + \sum_{i \notin \mathcal{S}} |a_i \sigma(\langle w_i, x \rangle + b_i)| \\ &\leq \frac{\epsilon}{2} + \frac{\epsilon}{2} = \epsilon \end{align*} Therefore, with probability \( 1 - \delta \): \[ \sup_{x \in U} \| h(x) - y(x) \| \leq \epsilon \]

□
Remark 2. The uniform distribution can be replaced by any continuous distribution on a closed interval. The uniform distribution is simply the most convenient choice.
Conclusion

I extended the results of [Lajoie & Mazzucato, 2025] to a broader class of activation functions — specifically bounded continuous sigmoidal functions — and proved the universal approximation theorem for these functions via the quasi bias-learning notion.

However, the same limitations from the original paper remain. In particular, my proof for sigmoidal functions relies strictly on continuity. One natural direction for improvement would be to replace continuous functions by \( L^p \) functions — but this remains an open question.
